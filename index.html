About Coach Ricky

FROM THE
FIELD TO
YOUR GAME.

In high school, Ricky was an Aflac All-American and was ranked by Perfect Game as the No. 2 third baseman in the nation and No. 18 first baseman nationally. His senior year he was drafted in the 24th round by the Boston Red Sox, but chose to pursue his college degree at the University of Southern California (USC).

Over three seasons at USC, Ricky posted a .331 batting average across 170 games with 167 starts. He is tied for fifth on USC's all-time career home run list with 40, sharing the honor with Morgan Ensberg (1995–98), and his 159 career RBIs place him fifth on USC's all-time list as well.

Ricky was selected in the 3rd round of the MLB Draft by the San Francisco Giants. He hit 37 home runs over parts of 4 seasons with the Double-A affiliate Richmond Flying Squirrels — the most in franchise history — and holds the franchise's all-time RBI record with 165.

Following his playing career, Ricky has remained deeply invested in the game as a hitting instructor and coach. He is passionate about passing his knowledge and love of baseball to today's youth. He now resides in South Orange County with his wife and two children, who enjoy the beach and camping.

Coaching Philosophy

PROCESS OVER
OUTCOME.

"Win the pitch, not the result." — You are not trying to get a hit. You are trying to take a great swing on a great pitch.

01
Define the Process
Every hitter controls three things regardless of age or level: their Approach (what pitch, where), their Timing (are you on time for the fastball?), and their Intent (are you swinging with conviction?)

Success means executing these — not getting a hit.
02
One Pitch at a Time
The game only happens in this pitch. Past swings don't matter. Future results don't exist yet. Every at-bat resets to zero. The only thing real is the pitch that's coming.
"This pitch. Right here."
03
Measure Success Differently
Stop asking "Did I get a hit?" Start asking: Did I swing at my pitch? Was I on time? Did I stay through the ball?

If yes — that's a win, regardless of what the scoreboard says.
04
Commit Fully
Hesitation kills hitters. You either attack or you take. There is no in-between. A half-committed swing is worse than no swing at all.
"See it. Trust it. Go."
05
Slow the Game Down
Good hitters control tempo mentally. Step out. Breathe. Reset. Build a consistent pre-pitch routine — pick a small target, take a deep breath, lock back in. Make the game come to you.
06
Handle Failure Right
Failure is guaranteed in hitting — even the best fail 7 out of 10 times. Don't react emotionally. Acknowledge it, flush it, and refocus. The next pitch doesn't care about the last one.
"Next pitch."
07
Build Real Confidence
Confidence doesn't come from results — it comes from reps. Quality BP, game-like practice, and repetition of your approach build the earned trust in your process that holds up when it matters.
08
Keep It Simple
For youth hitters: swing at good pitches, be on time, hit it hard. For advanced hitters: layer in pitch recognition, situational hitting, and adjustments. Complexity is earned — not assumed.
The Big Idea

The difference between pressing and performing isn't talent — it's where your mind is. Shift your focus from what you can't control to what you can, and performance follows naturally.
